Martha Meachum Chocolate Cake

This recipe was created by my grandmother Ginny Boykin Wheeler and enjoyed by my family for many, many years.

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 2 oz. chocolate
  • 2 cup flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 1/2 cup sugar
  • 3 eggs, separated
  • 1 tsp. soda
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  1. Cream butter and sugar; add melted chocolate and egg yolks.
  2. Alternately add sifted dry ingredients with buttermilk and vanilla.
  3. Begin and end with dry ingredients.
  4. Fold in beaten egg whites.
  5. Makes three layers.
  6. Bake about 25 minutes at 350°.

Martha Meachum Filling

The icing is very difficult to make, so follow the instructions carefully. Do not fret if you fail, as when you succeed, it will be very sweet.

  • 1 stick margarine
  • 1 cup whipping cream
  • 1 1/2 squares chocolate
  • 2 cup sugar
  • 2 Tbsp. Karo syrup
  1. Mix all the ingredients except for margarine in large heavy saucepan; stir only till the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Cook to softball stage.
    3. Softball Stage
    This stage can be determined by dropping a spoonful of hot syrup into a bowl of very cold water until it becomes a ball.
  3. Remove and add margarine.
  4. Partially cool, then beat till frosting loses its shine.
  5. Frost cake quickly.
